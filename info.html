Common Flowers & Plants

Here are some of the most well-known flowers and plants along with their basic care tips and identification features.

Flowers

Rose

Identification: Thorny stems, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and large, fragrant flowers in various colors.

Care Tips: Requires full sun, well-drained soil, deep but infrequent watering, and regular pruning.

Sunflower

Identification: Tall plants with thick, hairy stems, large rough leaves, and bright yellow flowers with a dark center.

Care Tips: Thrives in full sun, requires deep but occasional watering, and may need support for taller varieties.

Tulip

Identification: Smooth, long green leaves and cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring in various colors.

Care Tips: Prefers cool climates, well-draining soil, and light watering to prevent bulb rot.

Marigold

Identification: Bright orange or yellow flowers with a strong scent, bushy green foliage.

Care Tips: Needs full sun, moderate watering, and well-draining soil.

Lavender

Identification: Woody stems with narrow, silver-green leaves and small purple flowers with a strong fragrance.

Care Tips: Needs full sun, well-draining soil, minimal watering, and occasional pruning.

Hydrangea

Identification: Large, round clusters of flowers in shades of blue, pink, or white, with broad, serrated leaves.

Care Tips: Prefers partial sun, moist well-draining soil, and occasional pruning.

Plants

Cactus

Identification: Thick, fleshy stems that store water, often covered in spines or ridges.

Care Tips: Needs bright light, minimal watering, and sandy, well-draining soil.

Orchid

Identification: Thick, waxy leaves with aerial roots. Flowers come in various colors, often having a distinct lip petal.

Care Tips: Prefers indirect light, high humidity, and watering once a week.

Snake Plant

Identification: Upright, sword-like green leaves with yellow or white edges.

Care Tips: Tolerates low light, requires minimal watering, and helps purify indoor air.

Pothos

Identification: Vining plant with heart-shaped leaves, often green or variegated with white and yellow.

Care Tips: Thrives in low to bright indirect light, requires occasional watering, and benefits from occasional trimming.

Fern

Identification: Delicate, feathery fronds that grow in a variety of lush, green shades.

Care Tips: Prefers indirect light, high humidity, and consistently moist soil.

Basil

Identification: Green, aromatic leaves that are commonly used in cooking.

Care Tips: Requires full sun, regular watering, and well-draining soil.
